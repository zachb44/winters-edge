# Ghost Powers Game — Future Game Concept (Working Title)

A single-player action game built around a ghost power set. Original IP, inspired by the mechanics of Danny Phantom but not using any licensed characters or setting.

## Status

**Shower thought.** Not scoped, not planned. Captured so the idea doesn't disappear.

## Origin

Inspired by Danny Phantom (Nickelodeon) — a teen with ghost powers fighting supernatural threats. The show was never made into a proper standalone game. The power set is mechanically rich and underexplored in gaming.

**IP note:** Danny Phantom is owned by Paramount/Nickelodeon. Any game must use original characters, setting, and story. The inspiration is the *mechanics* (ghost powers as a gameplay system), not the brand.

## One-line pitch

**A teenager discovers ghost powers and must master invisibility, intangibility, possession, and combat to protect their city from supernatural threats.**

## Core fantasy

You're a normal person who gains ghost abilities. You phase through walls, turn invisible, possess enemies, fly, and blast ecto-energy. You're learning your powers while threats escalate. The city is your playground.

## Why this power set is interesting

Ghost powers are mechanically diverse in a way most game protagonists aren't:

- **Invisibility** — stealth system. Sneak past enemies, set up ambushes, eavesdrop for intel. Drains energy while active.
- **Intangibility / Phasing** — movement system. Walk through walls, drop through floors, dodge attacks by going incorporeal. Opens up verticality and non-linear level design.
- **Possession** — control system. Take over an enemy and use their abilities, access restricted areas, turn groups against each other. High risk (vulnerable while possessing).
- **Flight** — traversal system. Move freely in 3D space. City traversal like Spider-Man swinging but with ghost aesthetics (glowing trail, translucent form).
- **Ecto-blasts** — ranged combat. Energy projectiles, charged shots, area bursts. Scales with power level.
- **Ghost sense** — detection system. Alerts you to nearby supernatural threats. Could work like spider-sense or detective vision.

Most action games give you one traversal gimmick (web-swinging, grapple hook, flight). Ghost powers give you four (phase, fly, invisible, possess) that all interact differently with the environment.

## Power progression

- Start weak — invisibility flickers, phasing only works on thin walls, possession fails on strong-willed enemies
- Level up / skill tree unlocks longer duration, stronger effects, combo abilities
- Late game: phase + invisibility simultaneously, possess boss-tier enemies, ecto-shield, ghost clones
- Powers drain a shared energy pool — resource management under pressure

## Combat design ideas

- **Melee + ranged hybrid** — ecto-enhanced punches up close, energy blasts at range
- **Stealth takedowns** — invisibility + phase behind enemy + grab
- **Possession combat** — take over a tough enemy and use them to clear a room, then ditch the body
- **Environmental** — phase objects through walls to drop them on enemies, become intangible to let attacks pass through you and hit enemies behind you
- **Boss fights** — ghosts with their own power sets that counter yours. A boss who can see invisible targets forces you to fight head-on. A boss who phases forces you to use possession or traps.

## Setting ideas (original IP)

- Modern city with a supernatural underworld bleeding through
- "Ghost zone" alternate dimension layered on top of the real world (phase between them)
- Day = normal life (narrative, relationships, side quests), Night = ghost threats emerge
- Locations: school/work, downtown, industrial district, cemetery, abandoned facilities, the ghost zone itself

## Comparable games (mechanical, not IP)

- **Spider-Man PS4/PS5** — city traversal, power progression, combat flow
- **Infamous** — superpowered open world, moral choices, power upgrades
- **Dishonored** — stealth + supernatural powers, multiple approaches to objectives
- **Control** — supernatural agency, environmental destruction, weird atmosphere
- **Prototype** — body horror powers, possession-adjacent mechanics, city sandbox

Dishonored is probably the closest mechanical fit — its power set (blink, possession, dark vision, time stop) maps almost 1:1 to ghost powers but in a different aesthetic.

## Perspective

Third-person camera following the character (Spider-Man / Infamous style) is the natural fit. The power set demands seeing your character transform, phase, fly. Top-down doesn't serve this concept.

## Scope reality (honest)

This is the most ambitious concept in the future-games folder:
- 3D third-person action game
- Open world or semi-open (hub + missions)
- Complex power interactions with environment
- 3D models, animations, VFX for every power
- AI that reacts to stealth, possession, phasing
- City environment art

This is NOT a solo-with-Claude project in its full form. Options:
1. **Full vision** — needs a team, engine (Unreal/Unity), artists, significant budget. Years of work.
2. **Scaled down** — Metroidvania or roguelike with ghost powers in 2D. Phase through platforms, possess enemies in side-view combat, invisibility as a stealth mechanic. Buildable solo. Loses the Spider-Man traversal feel but keeps the power set.
3. **Prototype** — build a single room/level in Godot 3D to test if the core powers feel fun. No city, no progression, just: can you phase through a wall, possess a guard, and blast a ghost in a way that feels good?
