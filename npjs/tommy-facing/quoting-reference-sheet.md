# 📖 QUOTING — REFERENCE SHEET (read at home, NOT on a job)

**This is the get-familiar doc. Read it on the couch a few times so the answers are already in your head.**
**On a job you carry the GLANCE CARD, not this.** The glance card has the 5 steps, the rates, the floor, and the big 3 answers — that's the live tool. This sheet is everything behind it.

> Why two docs: the freeze happens live, under pressure. The live card can't hold the thing that causes the freeze (a wall of Q&As). So the full bank lives here, at home, where you've got time to read it.

## 🗣️ THE FULL ANSWER BANK (all 16 — read, don't memorize word-for-word)

The goal isn't to be perfect. It's to **not go blank.** Short, honest, confident wins. The 🔥 ones are the big 3 — they're on your glance card too because they're the ones that freeze you most.

### Price / scope

**🔥 1. "How much will it cost?"**
> "Depends on the size and surface — let me measure real quick and I'll give you a number right now." *(Then run the 5 steps. Wood/roof/complex: "Let me take a quick look so I quote it right instead of guessing.")*

**2. "Why is your price different from the guy who quoted $X?"**
> "Cheaper bids usually skip the protection — they don't pre-wet your plants or tape fixtures, and some use harsh pressure that can damage siding. My price is doing it right the first time."

**3. "Can you do my driveway and house at the same time?"**
> "Yeah — and it's cheaper to bundle than two separate trips. Want me to give you a combined number?"

**4. "Do you charge by the hour or the job?"**
> "By the job. I quote it up front so there are no surprises."

### Time / logistics

**5. "How long will it take?"**
> "Most homes are about 2–4 hours with setup and cleanup; driveways 1–2. Depends on size and buildup."

**6. "Do I need to be home?"**
> "No, as long as I've got water access and can get to the gate/pets."

**7. "Do you use my water? Will it spike my bill?"**
> "Yeah, I run off your outside spigot — usage is small, about like running a sink. Pennies on the bill."

**8. "What if it rains?"**
> "Light rain's fine. If it's unsafe we just reschedule, no charge."

### Damage / safety (the trust questions)

**🔥 9. "Will this damage my siding / wood / windows?"**
> "No — because I match the method to the surface. Your siding gets a soft wash — low pressure and a cleaning solution that does the work, not high pressure. I only use real pressure on concrete. That's how you avoid damage."

**10. "Are you going to pressure wash my roof?"**
> "No — roofs need a low-pressure soft-wash treatment, never high pressure; that strips the shingles." *(Job-one honest version: "Roofs actually aren't a service I offer yet.")*

**11. "Will the bleach kill my plants / hurt my pets?"**
> "I pre-wet and rinse all your plants, and the mix is diluted and controlled. Keep pets inside while I work — they're fine once everything dries."

**🔥 12. "Are you insured?"** *(your real gap until the GL policy is filed — Bucket D)*
> "I'm owner-operated and I'm careful — I protect your plants, fixtures, and windows before I start. I'm in the process of getting my general liability set up." Then move the conversation to the work.
> ⚠️ **Do NOT claim coverage that doesn't exist.** This is a bridge line, not the fix. The fix is filing the GL policy — a customer asking this on a live quote is the signal to get it done.

### Results / longevity

**13. "How long will it stay clean / will the green come back?"**
> "A soft wash kills the growth at the root, so it lasts about 1–2 years. Plain water just rinses the surface and it comes back fast. Shade and trees shorten it."

**14. "Why does the mold keep coming back?"**
> "Water alone doesn't kill the spores — you need the right solution and time to sit. I also look at what's feeding it: shade, gutters, drainage."

**15. "How often should I get this done?"**
> "In this humidity, houses about every 1–2 years — yearly if you've got heavy shade or trees. Driveways about yearly."

**16. "Can you get this specific stain out?"** (oil, rust, red clay, paint)
> "Most come out. Oil, rust, and heavy clay need special treatment and might cost a little more or take a second pass. Some paint overspray may not fully come out — I'll be straight with you after I see it." *(Don't over-promise.)*

## 🪵 THE IN-PERSON-ONLY SURFACES (never quote these by phone)

**Decks · fences · wood · pool decks.** No reliable local flat rate exists, and condition swings the price hard (graying, old stain, soft spots). Wood is also the surface most likely to get damaged by a beginner and most likely to spark a "you'll need to re-stain after" conversation.

- **Always look in person before you give a number.** Start estimate ~$0.20–0.40/sq ft, but the in-person look is non-negotiable.
- **The line:** "Let me take a quick look so I quote it right, not guess."
- **Pool decks:** treat like flatwork on sq ft, but the coating (Kool Deck/textured) needs a gentler touch — look first.

**Phone-quotable (on the glance card):** driveways, standard house washes. Everything else = look first.

## 🛑 THE FULL FLOOR MATH (the glance card has the short version)

Your "$60/hr" is **not $60 in your pocket.** Out of every job comes gas, chemicals, wear, and your real time — including drive, setup, prep/taping, the wash, AND cleanup. So the real question is never "what's my hourly" — it's **"what does this job NET me per hour of my life, door to door."**

### The 3 steps

> **Step 1 — Honest time, door to door.** Not just spray time. Drive + setup + prep + wash + cleanup. Most home washes = 2–4 hrs. Driveways = 1–2 hrs. **When unsure, estimate HIGH** — under-estimating time is exactly how you end up at $20/hr.
>
> **Step 2 — Subtract job costs.** Chemicals + gas for that job. Normal job ≈ $20–40 in materials/gas. Heavy-growth jobs eat more chemical — bump it.
>
> **Step 3 — Net ÷ hours = your real hourly.** (Quote − job costs) ÷ honest hours. THAT is what you actually made.

### The floor line (the walk-away rule)

> **Below ~$50/hr NET of your real door-to-door time → STOP. Re-quote or walk.**
>
> **$60/hr is your TARGET on the meter. ~$50/hr NET is the floor.** Net means after gas, chemicals, and wear — not the sticker price. $60 on the meter is above the floor; that's the point. The floor exists for the moment you're tempted to say a low number to win a job: a job that nets under $50/hr is costing you, not paying you.

### Worked examples

- **Standard house wash, 2,000 sf, light growth, one story.** Quote $375 (mid of $300–450). Door-to-door ~3.5 hrs. Costs ~$30. Net = $345 ÷ 3.5 = **~$98/hr.** Well above floor — say it confidently.
- **Two-car driveway, oil stains, easy access.** Base $200 + $50 oil = $250. ~2 hrs. Costs ~$25. Net = $225 ÷ 2 = **~$112/hr.** Above floor. Good.
- **The trap job:** 2,500 sf two-story, heavy growth, lots of landscaping — tempted to say $300 to win it. Real door-to-door ~6 hrs. Costs ~$45. Net = $255 ÷ 6 = **~$42/hr. BELOW FLOOR.** This is the exact job that pays you $20–40/hr if you're not careful. Correct move: it's a **Large** job ($500–800), two-story (+30–50%), heavy growth (+20–40%). Re-quote **$550–650**, or don't take it.

## 🚧 THE FULL "TOO BIG FOR ONE PERSON" TEST

Recognize the job you can't do solo BEFORE you say yes. **If 2+ of these are true, it's probably not a one-person job** — "let me bring a hand" or "let me look first" beats a yes you regret:

- [ ] **Two-plus stories** with peaks you can't reach safely from the ground
- [ ] **Roof involved** (not a service yet — automatic "not now")
- [ ] **Big commercial-size flatwork** (parking lot, huge lot) — a full day-plus solo
- [ ] **Heavy growth on a large home** — the time doubles and the floor math gets ugly fast
- [ ] **Anything needing a ladder + wand at the same time** (never spray off a ladder)
- [ ] **A timeline you can't hit alone** ("can you do all this by Saturday?")

> **The honest line when it's too big:** "That's a bigger job than I want to rush solo — let me either bring a hand or take a real look and quote it right."
> Sizing a job honestly isn't weakness. Saying yes to a job that breaks you is the actual risk.
