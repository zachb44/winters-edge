## Who you're helping

I'm Zach. My OLDER brother Tommy (Thomas Bellar Jr.) is launching a handyman
business, NPJS LLC (No Problems Just Solutions), in Columbia SC. He has strong,
unregulated ADHD. His obstacle is **execution and consistency, not knowledge or
work ethic.** He's trusted me to help quarterback this, which for an older brother
is a humble thing — keep the tone respectful of that.

Foundation-first rule: we are deliberately starting with **ONE service line — power
washing.** Not the whole handyman trade. This research is power washing ONLY. Do not
expand scope into carpentry, drywall, painting, etc., even though Tommy can do those.
Those are Phase 2. One bullet first.

Why power washing is the first bullet: a friend (Bobby) is lending Tommy a washer
(zero equipment cost), it's the easiest service to quote (priced by surface/sq ft,
not guesswork), the before/after results sell the next job, and it can be done out
of his current vehicle today with no truck.

Cash is tight (~$1K to his name). Every recommendation factors real budget reality.

## What I need you to research

Rip through many current sources — don't wait for my permission between searches.
I want dense, actionable output, not a survey.

### Pricing must be REAL and LOCAL — this is the hard requirement

This research feeds the quoting engine (Bucket C), which builds Tommy's protective
hourly floor on YOUR pricing numbers. If the pricing is generic national content-farm
data (HomeAdvisor / Angi / Thumbtack national ranges), the floor will be wrong in the
exact market where Tommy operates, and he'll under-quote himself into working for
$20/hr. So:

- **Find and cite at least 2–3 ACTUAL Columbia / Midlands-SC power washing operators'
  real rates.** Sources: their own websites, Google Business profiles, local Facebook
  groups / Nextdoor quote threads, Yelp quote mentions, published service-area pricing.
  Name them or note the source. Real local numbers, not national averages.
- **For every surface type below, include a labeled "Columbia reality vs. national
  average" line** — state where the local Columbia/Midlands market diverges from the
  US average (higher, lower, same) and by roughly how much.
- **If you genuinely cannot find local data for a given surface, FLAG that gap
  explicitly** ("no Columbia-specific data found for soft-wash roofs; national range
  used as placeholder — confirm locally before quoting"). Do NOT silently substitute
  a national number and present it as if it were local. The flag is the deliverable
  when local data is missing.

### The research items

1. **Current price ranges** by job type and surface (real/local per the rule above):
   - Driveways / concrete (per sq ft AND typical flat-job range)
   - House siding — vinyl, brick, stucco
   - Decks / wood / fencing
   - Patios, walkways, pool decks
   - Roofs (soft wash — flag if this needs special skill/risk)
   - Note what a typical small/medium/large job actually bills out at

2. **What separates a $150 job from a $400+ job** — size, prep, two-story access,
   chemical needs, heavy staining/mold. So Tommy can SEE the variables that move price.

3. **Technique & safety basics per surface** — PSI ranges, when to use soft wash vs
   pressure, what damages what (wood gouging, stripping vinyl, etching concrete,
   cracking windows). The goal: Tommy does not destroy a customer's property on job one.

4. **Supplies/consumables he'll actually buy** with rough costs — detergents/surface
   cleaners, the surface-cleaner attachment, nozzle tips, safe chemicals (e.g. sodium
   hypochlorite mixes), basic safety gear. What does a realistic starter kit cost on
   top of the borrowed washer?

5. **The curveball questions customers ask** when getting a power washing quote.
   This list feeds Bucket C's anti-freeze "if-they-ask-X-say-Y" bank, so completeness
   matters — aim for at least 12–15 real questions, not a handful. These are the
   questions that freeze Tommy live and lose him the job.

6. **Common beginner mistakes** that lose money or damage property or burn the
   customer relationship.
