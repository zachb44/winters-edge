## Who you're helping

I'm Zach — CTO/co-founder at FT3 Capital, founder of ZCB LLC (sales/ops + AI operator
work). My OLDER brother Tommy (Thomas Bellar Jr.) is launching a handyman business,
NPJS LLC, in Columbia SC, starting with power washing as the only service. Strong
unregulated ADHD. His obstacle is execution and consistency, not knowledge. He's
trusted me — his younger brother — to help build this, which takes humility. Tone
stays brother-first, never condescending.

This is NOT a research chat. It's a translation job: take MY real operating systems
and strip them down hard into something a box-in-the-closet brain will actually use.

## The gap this fills

From the call that started this: Tommy has a clear mental model of DOING JOBS but
almost no model of RUNNING A BUSINESS. He described his paperwork system as "throw
the mail in a box, put it in the closet" (literally). He said "I don't do paperwork
and government stuff." There's no day structure, no money system, no way for him to
know if a week was good. That's the hole. Fill it — but fill it ADHD-proof.

## My real systems — use as the reference model, then STRIP HARD

These work for me; Tommy needs a much simpler version:

- **Money funnel:** everything lands in the business account first. The MOMENT it
  hits, I pull 20% into a separate tax account I keep hidden from my own view (my
  CPA Uncle Ron set the 20% figure). I never have to "remember" to set aside taxes.
- **Bill calendar:** most bills cluster around the 10th-15th. Top of month I move a
  fixed lump (~$1,500) that covers the month's bills. Around the 25th-26th I move
  another lump for the next batch / my half of the mortgage.
- **Push-not-pull:** I verbalize my own deadlines out loud so my transcript system
  auto-populates them in front of me. If it's out of sight, it's forgotten forever.
  Every system I build pushes the important thing INTO my face. I never rely on
  "remember to check."
- **ADHD guardrails:** that's literally a file name built into my tools. The principle
  is: I suck at executive/admin stuff, so I automate it away and surface only the one
  thing that matters, when it matters.

Tommy is MORE constrained than me and has no medication, no boss, minimal bills
(lives at Dad's). So his version must be even simpler. Anything requiring "remember
to," multiple fields, or multi-step updates is DEAD ON ARRIVAL. Single surface, push
not pull, no maintenance.

## How hard "strip hard" actually means — pass/fail tests

"Simpler than Zach's system" is the wrong yardstick — four steps feels simple next to
my full setup and is still too much for Tommy. Use these binary tests instead. If a
deliverable fails one, it's not stripped enough — rebuild it:

- **Money system FAILS** if Tommy has to track more than ONE number day-to-day.
- **Job lifecycle FAILS** if knowing a job's stage requires any update step Tommy
  has to initiate (moving a card, editing a field, opening an app).
- **Tracking surface FAILS** if using it requires opening anything that isn't already
  open / already in his physical line of sight.
- **Anything FAILS** if a step begins with the words "remember to." Find every
  "remember to" and delete it — replace it with something that happens automatically
  or sits permanently in view.
- **Push mechanism reminder:** Tommy has no transcript system like mine. His "push"
  is allowed to be low-tech — the Sunday 3-number check-in from the plan (Zach texts/
  calls), a single card in the truck, one thing stuck by the door. Don't invent an app;
  the seed already forbids it. Use the human + physical push that already exists.

## What to build

1. **Day-in-the-life structure** — what a working day actually looks like. Morning
   = comms/quotes, midday = job sites, Friday = wrap-ups and quoting only (never
   START a job Friday — his own rule from the call). Make it concrete, not poetic.

2. **Money movement, Tommy-scale** — the simplest possible version of my funnel.
   Money in → tax cut off the top → bills → what's left. He has almost no bills right
   now, so this is more about building the HABIT before the money's big than managing
   complexity. Route the tax % decision to Uncle Ron. (Remember the one-number test.)

3. **Job lifecycle** — lead → quote → scheduled → in progress → done → paid. How does
   he know which stage a job is in WITHOUT a CRM and without maintaining anything?
   Dead simple. He explicitly won't use Jobber-type apps right now ($ and overhead).
   (Remember the no-self-initiated-update test.)

4. **The "is this week good?" measure** — formalize the 3-number Sunday check-in from
   the plan: jobs booked/completed, cash on hand, the one stuck thing. That's the whole
   accountability system. Don't add to it.

5. **Tracking that survives ADHD** — one surface, push not pull. Explicitly NOT a
   spreadsheet he has to maintain, NOT an app with fields. Think: the one thing that
   shows him what matters without him hunting for it. If you propose a tool, it must
   be near-zero maintenance. (Remember the already-in-view test.)
