**From Bucket B Rev 3 (money decoupled from the Sunday call), Jun 24 2026:**

- **Why Rev 3 happened:** Rev 2 made the system robust by welding the tax set-aside and stuck-job sweep
  to a weekly Sunday call. That turned a warm brother-to-brother call into a business-critical hub and
  created a single point of failure (skip the call → taxes silently stop). Rev 3 removes that SPOF by
  tying the money mechanic to the money itself, not to a conversation:
  1. **Tax split moved off the call, onto the cash.** The truck envelope is now a ONE-WAY box. The only
     way to take money out is to empty the whole thing, and emptying it triggers the split: empty → tax
     20% off the top → what's left is MAIN (spendable). The trigger is Tommy wanting his own money — a
     want he already has — not a call Zach has to make. A pinch valve keeps the one-way rule absolute:
     if he must pull cash in a pinch, he empties + splits right then, never takes a twenty and leaves the
     rest.
  2. **Stuck-job sweep moved off the call, onto the cards.** A stuck card is physically in his face on
     the visor every drive — it nags on its own, no call required. The call (if it happens) is a friendly
     second catch, not the mechanism.
  3. **Sunday call reframed as relational only.** Three light questions, no prep, nothing operational
     rides on it. If a week is missed, NOTHING breaks — taxes already happened at the money, stuck jobs
     already nag from the visor. They just catch up next week.
- **The Rev 2 single point of failure is REMOVED.** There is no longer a "the business breaks if Zach
  doesn't call" dependency. The residual risk is smaller and sits on Tommy following the empty → tax-off-
  top → spend order, which rides on his own appetite for the cash (lowest-failure point available).
- **Killed task (was here under Rev 2):** "Put the recurring Sunday call in Zach's own push system." No
  longer load-bearing — it was a fix for the SPOF that Rev 3 removes. The Sunday call is now just a call;
  Zach texts/calls Tommy on Sundays-ish as brother stuff, and nothing financial or operational depends on
  it happening on any given week.
